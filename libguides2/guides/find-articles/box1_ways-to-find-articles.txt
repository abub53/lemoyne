<h4>Not sure what database will fit your topic best?</h4>

<ul>
  <li>Try browsing the <strong><a href="/guides/subjects">Subject Guides</a></strong> which identify key databases and other research starting points.</li>
  <li>Search a full-text database such as <strong><a href="https://0-search.proquest.com.library.lemoyne.edu/central" target="_blank">ProQuest Central</a></strong> or <strong><a href="http://0-search.ebscohost.com.library.lemoyne.edu/login.aspx?authtype=ip,uid&amp;profile=ehost&amp;defaultdb=afh" target="_blank">Academic Search Elite</a></strong> that cover many topics.</li>
</ul>

<h4>Have an article citation (partial or full) or just a journal title?</h4>

<ul>
  <li>If you have a fairly complete citation, try entering the information into the <strong><a href="http://wp8kk7mz7x.search.serialssolutions.com/?SS_Page=refiner&amp;SS_RefinerEditable=yes" target="_blank">Citation Linker</a></strong>. If the Library has access to the article, this service will attempt to find it for you.</li>
  <li>Search the <em><strong>journal title</strong></em> in the <strong><a href="http://wp8kk7mz7x.search.serialssolutions.com/">Journal Finder</a></strong>. Then look for a link to a full-text provider service that will link you to the contents of the e-journal. If the journal is a title we have in print, head to the shelf.</li>
</ul>
