<h4 style="border-bottom: 1px solid #a2a2a2; padding: 4px 0">Group Study Rooms</h4>

<p><strong>Four Group Study Rooms</strong> located on the second floor are available during <a href="http://resources.library.lemoyne.edu/library/hours" target="_blank">library hours</a>. See: <a href="http://resources.library.lemoyne.edu/pubserv-rooms/study-rooms">Reserve a Room</a> guide for directions and a more complete description of these rooms.</p>

<p>Each room contains electrical outlets, wireless internet access, and dry-erase boards. Dry-erase markers may be requested at the Library Services Desk at the front of the library. Laptops may be borrowed from the IT Service Desk on the first floor of the library. Study Room A may also be equpped with a video monitor and DVD/VHS player.</p>

<p>Study Room A accommodates 8-10 people. Study Rooms B, C, and D each accommodate 2-5 people.</p>

<p>You may book any Group Study Room (or a combination of rooms) in half-hour increments for up to <strong>3 hours per day per person</strong>. It is also acceptable for several people in the same study group to coordinate bookings to book the room longer than the 3 hour per person limit. Please do not monopolize or abuse this privilege.</p>

<p>Confirmed bookings for each day are listed by clicking the &quot;Confirmed Bookings&quot; button at the top right of this page.</p>

<ul>
  <li>Rooms must be reserved at least two hours in advance.</li>
  <li>If not reserved, rooms may be used on a first-come, first-served basis.</li>
  <li>Please do not reserve a room unless you are certain you will use it.</li>
  <li>Please do not reserve a group study room for individual use.</li>
  <li>Use the short form of your email address for bookings -- i.e., @lemoyne.edu rather than @stu.lemoyne.edu.</li>
  <li>The rooms are not class space and should not be booked on a recurring basis (daily, weekly, monthly, etc.)</li>
  <li>Please leave the room in the same condition you found it.</li>
  <li>Do not leave personal belongings or study materials unattended. Theft of valuable items is often a crime of opportunity.</li>
</ul>

<p>See also: Request forms for booking <a href="/reserve/individual-study-rooms">Individual Study Rooms</a> and <a href="/reserve/media-viewing-rooms">Media Viewing Rooms</a></p>

<p>If you need to reserve the Muserlian Instructional Lab, visit the <a href="http://resources.library.lemoyne.edu/library-services/room-reservation/lab">Reserve a Room</a> guide.</p>
