Library's QuickSearch Help

What is QuickSearch?

Library's QuickSearch allows you to access several electronic resources in a single step. QuickSearch provides citations, abstracts, full-text articles (as available) and books in our library.

Search Tips

Use the Basic search page to enter a simple search.
Use the Advanced search page for searching more than one term with Boolean* operators.
* Try using Boolean operators (AND, OR, and NOT) to improve your search. Phrase searching works too! Put "quotation marks" around your search phrase to retrieve more precise results.

Search Results

You will see your first results. You can click on the number to view the results while the remaining databases continue searching.

Clustered Results

Clustered results sorts the results into categories. Clustering can also give you new ideas for your search query.

Sort Options

All sorting and filtering can be done in the header above your results:
  • Sort Results by: These links allow you to sort your search results by publication date, title, author, or source. If your results contain publication dates, they will be automatically sorted by date unless you choose otherwise.
  • Filter by full-text or peer-reviewed content, or to both.

New or Refined Search

At the top of the page, above your clustered results, are "Refine Search" and "New Search" links.
  • Refine Search: This is where you can edit and run the search again.
  • New Search: This takes you to a fresh search page.

Next Results

Because results are returned in small batches, on the final page of results, you can click on the "Next Results" link to see the next batch of results.
The new set will automatically follow the original results set. Use the "merge" icon if you prefer to combine all results into one list. This sorts the list according to your selected method or if you don't sort, results are by date.

Export, Save, and Email

There are links to email, export/save at the top of the citation list. To select records, use the checkboxes next to each result.
Clicking on "selected records" allows you to view selected citations in a new windows.

Clicking on "Email", you may choose a format, such as EndNote or ProCite.

Clicking on "Export/Save" allows you to export your selected records directly to RefWorks, or to save the records in a format, such as EndNote or ProCite.
